Slide 7 · BOLA vs BFLA
There's a similar one. Here's the difference.
BOLA and BFLA are cousins — easy to confuse, important to keep separate.
BOLA · API1
Broken Object Level Authorization
You access the wrong object
Same role, different user's data
Example: You're user 1042. You see user 1041's order.
Both users are regular customers — you just got their stuff
BFLA · API5
Broken Function Level Authorization
You access the wrong function
Lower role accesses higher-privilege actions
Example: You're a regular user. You call the admin endpoint.
You shouldn't have that power at all — it's a role violation
The easy way to remember it

BOLA = accessing someone else's stuff (wrong object, same level)

BFLA = accessing actions you shouldn't have (wrong function, wrong level)

BOLA is about what you can see. BFLA is about what you can do.

Why it matters

BOLA is API1 — the most common. BFLA is API5. Both are authorization failures. This module covers BOLA. You'll see BFLA in a later module.
